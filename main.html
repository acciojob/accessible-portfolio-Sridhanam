Accessibility in digital design isn’t just about ticking boxes on a compliance checklist — it’s about ensuring that every user, regardless of their abilities, can access and interact with digital content. This includes users with visual, auditory, cognitive, motor, and other types of disabilities. For me, this isn’t just professional; it’s personal. My own experiences with visual impairment have heightened my awareness, but they’ve also opened my eyes to the broader spectrum of accessibility needs. When I decided to audit my own portfolio, the stakes felt especially high because I wanted carrusel.io to be truly inclusive for all users.
